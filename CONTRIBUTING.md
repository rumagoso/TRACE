# Contributing to TRACE

TRACE is an open proposal. It is not finished — it is designed to be improved by people who see the problem clearly and have something concrete to add.

---

## What Contributions Are Welcome

**Conceptual review**
Does the four-layer architecture hold? Are there failure modes not addressed? Are there existing standards or initiatives this conflicts with or should reference?

**Technical depth**
Layer 1 (Identity) and Layer 4 (Economic Alignment) have open problems explicitly deferred to working group:
- Precise definition of "Verified Failure" across jurisdictions
- Cross-jurisdictional enforceability of collateral release
- Interoperability specification between ledger implementations (EBSI, Hyperledger Fabric, others)

Concrete proposals for any of these are welcome.

**Jurisdiction and regulatory mapping**
TRACE currently maps primarily to EU regulatory context (EU AI Act, NIS2, EBSI). Equivalent mappings for US, UK, APAC, and other jurisdictions are needed. If you can write the mapping, open an issue or submit a pull request.

**Use case evidence**
Real-world scenarios where TRACE would have prevented a failure, or where the current architecture would not work as designed. Concrete beats theoretical.

**Governance design**
The TRACE Foundation Founding Cohort criteria are defined. The operational governance model — voting procedures, dispute resolution, amendment processes — is not. If you have experience designing multi-stakeholder governance (ICANN, W3C, Linux Foundation), your input is directly relevant.

---

## How to Contribute

1. **Open an issue** — for questions, challenges, or proposals you want to discuss before writing
2. **Submit a pull request** — for concrete edits, additions, or corrections to the position paper or supporting documents
3. **Email the author** — for substantive engagement, research collaboration, or institutional interest: rui.soares@crossjoin.pt

---

## What Is Not Appropriate

- Proposals to make TRACE a proprietary or vendor-controlled standard
- Contributions that remove the economic accountability layer (Layer 4) — this is a design requirement, not a feature
- Changes that narrow scope to enterprise-internal agents only — that problem is already being addressed by NCCoE and others
